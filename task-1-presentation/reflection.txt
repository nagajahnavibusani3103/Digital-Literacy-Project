Task 1 Reflection – Digital Literacy Infographic

While working on the digital literacy infographic, I understood that presenting information visually is very different from writing it in paragraphs. The main goal was to make the content simple, clear, and easy to understand at a glance. This required careful selection of words and proper organization of ideas.

Initially, I found it challenging to decide how to divide the content into sections without making the infographic look crowded. I had to think about what information was most important and how to present it in a limited space. I also focused on maintaining a balance between content and design so that it looks visually appealing but not overloaded with text.

Through this task, I learned the importance of structuring information properly. I realized that using short points, headings, and spacing makes the content more readable and engaging. I also understood how tools like Canva can be used effectively to create professional-looking designs without requiring advanced technical skills.

This activity improved my creativity and helped me think from the perspective of the viewer. Instead of just adding information, I focused on how others would understand it. Overall, this task helped me develop both my design skills and my ability to communicate ideas clearly using visuals.
